Why Bora Bora?

The island that exceeds all expectations

Bora Bora is that image everyone has in mind: an impossibly turquoise lagoon, overwater bungalows suspended above the water, and in the background, the volcanic silhouette of Mount Otemanu. And the reality? It surpasses even the photos.

Located in the Society Islands 270 km northwest of Tahiti, the main island is surrounded by a lagoon protected by a coral reef and a crown of motus (sandy islets). Here, sea turtles swim alongside manta rays in waters of exceptional clarity.

Our local take: Bora Bora is expensive, undeniably. But with the right strategy (off-season, family guesthouses, direct activities), it's possible to live the experience without breaking the bank.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions about Bora Bora

When is the best time to visit Bora Bora? +
The dry season from May to October is ideal: little rain, mild temperatures around 27°C, calm sea. July-August are the busiest but most beautiful months.
What budget should I plan for Bora Bora? +
Budget at least €2,500/person for a week on a budget (guesthouse + flights). For overwater bungalows at major resorts, plan €6,000 to €15,000 per person.
How to get to Bora Bora from France? +
Flight Paris-Tahiti (22h with stopover, Air France or Air Tahiti Nui), then connection on Air Tahiti to Bora Bora (45 min). Bora Bora airport is on a motu — a boat shuttle takes you to your hotel.
How long should I stay in Bora Bora? +
Minimum 4 nights to settle in and enjoy. Ideally 7 nights if your budget allows. Combine with Moorea or Tahiti for a complete circuit.
Do I need a visa for Bora Bora? +
No. French Polynesia is a French territory: EU nationals need no visa. A valid ID card is enough for French nationals.
