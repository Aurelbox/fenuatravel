Our local take

Tahiti beyond the clichés

Tahiti is often reduced to its role as a connecting airport for Bora Bora. This is a mistake. Polynesia's main island is actually a fascinating destination: a South Pacific metropolis with a sophisticated culinary scene, markets bursting with color, and lush countryside crossed by waterfalls and volcanic valleys.

In Tahiti, you experience Polynesian life at its most authentic. Papeete market at dawn, the roulotes food trucks on the waterfront at night, women in floral dresses, surf champions at Teahupo'o... And that light, that warmth, that scent of tiare everywhere. Tahiti takes some effort, but never disappoints.

Must-do

Best activities in Tahiti

Teahupo'o — The Olympic wave
Watch from a boat or beginner surf lessons on Tahiti Iti's calmer waves. Paris 2024 Olympic site.
Boat tour: 8,000 XPF
Faarumai Waterfalls
Three spectacular waterfalls deep in the rainforest, 30 min from Papeete. Free entry, swimming possible.
Free
Papeete Market
The heartbeat of local life. Vanilla, tiare, pareos, black pearls, exotic fruits and fresh fish. Visit early morning.
Free (entry)
Venus Point
The historic lighthouse where Cook observed Venus's transit in 1769. Black sand beach and spectacular views of Moorea.
Free
Tahiti and the Islands Museum
Exceptional collection on Polynesian culture and history, from prehistory to European navigators.
600 XPF / adult
Island road trip
The coastal road circles all of Tahiti Nui (130 km) with beaches, maraes, gardens and authentic villages.
Car rental: 7,000 XPF/day

FAQ

FAQ about Tahiti

Is there anything to do in Tahiti beyond the airport?+
Yes! Tahiti is often underestimated. The island offers the Teahupo'o wave, Faarumai waterfalls, the Museum of Tahiti and the Islands, Papeete market, Venus Point, and a remarkable food scene.
Can you surf in Tahiti?+
Yes, Tahiti is world-renowned for Teahupo'o, one of the most powerful waves on the planet and the Paris 2024 Olympic site. For beginners, calmer beaches exist in Tahiti Iti.
How many days to spend in Tahiti?+
2 to 3 days suffice to see the essentials. A week allows exploring the east coast, Teahupo'o, waterfalls and Tahiti Iti beaches.
Do you need a visa for Tahiti?+
No, French Polynesia is a French territory. EU citizens don't need a visa. A valid passport or national ID card is sufficient.
What's the best hotel in Tahiti?+
The InterContinental Tahiti Resort & Spa is the largest resort with lagoon access and bungalows. For value, the Tahiti Pearl Beach Resort and Manava Suite Resort are excellent alternatives.
What is the best time to visit Tahiti?+
The dry season, May to October, is ideal: mild temperatures (25-27°C), little rain, pleasant trade winds. It's also whale season (July-November) and Heiva festival time (July). The austral summer (November-April) is hotter and more humid.
How much is a Paris–Tahiti flight?+
Expect €800 to €1,800 in economy class depending on season and booking time. Air Tahiti Nui offers direct flights from Paris CDG (21h30 flight time). Air France, Corsair and Air Caraïbes also operate the route via Los Angeles. Book 3 to 6 months ahead for the best fares.
Where to eat local food in Tahiti?+
The food trucks (roulottes) along Papeete's waterfront (quai de la Marine) are unmissable for coconut milk raw fish, Chinese stir-fries and grilled skewers from 6pm onwards. Papeete market also has Polynesian snacks in the morning. Food truck budget: 1,000-1,500 XPF per dish.

💡 Local tip
Don't skip Tahiti! Arrive the night before your inter-island flight, sleep near the airport, then get up at 5am for Papeete market. Vendors set up their stalls at dawn and the atmosphere is unforgettable — women in flowers, scent of tiare, glistening fresh fish.
