Our local take

Moorea, Polynesia's best-kept secret

From the heights of the Belvedere, the view over Cook's Bay and Opunohu Bay takes your breath away: two intense blue inlets carved into black basalt, framed by dizzying forest-covered mountains. Moorea may well be the most beautiful island in French Polynesia — yet it remains in Bora Bora's shadow.

Just 17 km from Papeete, accessible in 30 minutes by ferry, it offers everything travelers seek in Polynesia: an exceptionally marine-rich lagoon, hikes through lush jungle, luxury resorts and family pensions, all in an atmosphere infinitely more authentic and affordable than Bora Bora.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions about Moorea

How to get to Moorea from Tahiti? +
Two options: the Aremiti or Moorea Ferry (30-45 min, ~1,000 XPF) from Papeete dock, or Air Tahiti flight (10 min, ~4,500 XPF). The ferry is perfectly adequate, scenic and economical.
What's the best time to visit Moorea? +
The dry season from May to October is ideal. July-August are the most popular months. Avoid December-March (rainy season and possible cyclones).
Can you swim with dolphins in Moorea? +
Yes, Moorea is one of the world's best destinations for swimming with wild dolphins. Boat tours allow you to observe them in their natural habitat. You can also swim with blacktip reef sharks and stingrays in the lagoon.
Do you need a vehicle in Moorea? +
Yes, the bus network is nearly non-existent. Rent a scooter (3,000-4,000 XPF/day) or car (7,000-10,000 XPF/day) to explore the island. Many hotels include bikes or offer rentals.
Is Moorea worth it compared to Bora Bora? +
Absolutely! Moorea is often considered more authentic and much more affordable than Bora Bora. Its lagoon is just as beautiful, with the added advantage of proximity to Tahiti and a more present local life.
